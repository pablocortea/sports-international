Ayase Ueda hits hat-trick

Japan crushes Myanmar in World Cup Qualifying Round

Hitori Tanawaka | 13:44 GMT-5
Share: |

Myanmar, having faced a 10-0 defeat in their prior clash with Japan during the 2022 World Cup qualifiers, adopted a defensive stance, rarely penetrating Japan's defensive line. Early on, Minamino missed a chance but swiftly made amends by lofting a precise ball for Ueda, who headed it past Myanmar's goalkeeper Kyaw Zin Phyo

Playing in a deeper midfield role under manager Hajime Moriyasu, Daichi Kamada from Lazio doubled Japan's lead, calmly steering a well-controlled shot into the net in the 28th minute. Ueda secured a brace with a clever finish from an acute angle, capitalizing on Ritsu Doan's pass into the right side of the box.

Ueda received applause from the home fans when he exited the field in the 66th minute, reflecting on the match: "My primary aim was victory. I believe I showcased some of my strengths as well," said the 25-year-old striker, in his inaugural season with Dutch champions following his move from Belgian side Cercle Brugge.

The scoring was sealed by Doan in the 86th minute, controlling Hidemasa Morita's long pass and firing past Kyaw. The Samurai Blue extended their winning streak to seven consecutive international matches, gearing up to face Syria in their upcoming World Cup qualifier in Jeddah, Saudi Arabia, scheduled for Tuesday.

The top two teams from qualifying groups will progress to the third phase of Asian World Cup qualifying.
