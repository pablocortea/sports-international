Kylian Mbappe says he has not made up mind on future

Richard A. Spencer | 03:25 GMT-5
Share: |

Paris St-Germain forward Kylian Mbappe has stated that he hasn't made a definitive decision regarding his future. The 25-year-old France captain's contract with the Ligue 1 champions is set to expire in the summer, leading to intense speculation linking him with a move to Real Madrid.

Mbappe, after informing PSG in June about not opting for an additional 12 months in his contract, addressed the matter saying, "First of all, I'm very, very, very motivated for this year. It's very important."

Following his goal-scoring performance for PSG in their 2-0 victory over Toulouse to secure the French Champions Trophy, he continued, "As I said, we've got titles to go after and we've already won one, so that's already done. After that, no, I haven't made up my mind yet."

div> Mbappe clarified, "But in any case, with the agreement I made with the chairman [Nasser Al-Khelaifi] this summer, it doesn't matter what I decide. We managed to protect all parties and preserve the club's serenity for the challenges ahead, which remains the most important thing. So we'll say it's secondary."

The forward, who initially joined PSG on loan from Monaco in 2017 before a record-breaking 180m euro ($197.2m) move, was slated to depart on a free transfer at the end of the 2021-22 season. However, he eventually signed a two-year contract extension, with the option for an additional year.

However, after declining the additional year, Mbappe wasn't part of PSG's pre-season tour to Japan. He also declined to meet with representatives from Al-Hilal, who made a world record $329m offer for him.

Following "very constructive and positive" talks, Mbappe returned to first-team training, as stated by PSG. Reflecting on his decision-making process, Mbappe mentioned, "If I know what I want to do, why drag it out? It just doesn't make sense."

Meanwhile, Barcelona manager Xavi acknowledged that players like Mbappe are beyond the Catalan club's financial reach at present. "Unfortunately, we are not in a privileged situation economically to do that. I am left with the players I have, and with those players we have to win," said Xavi.
