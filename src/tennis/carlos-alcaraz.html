CLAIMS TO BE FOCUSED ON CINCINNATI

Alcaraz targets overall improvement after Paul shock

Winston Aubergey | 03:12 GMT-5
Share: |

Carlos Alcaraz acknowledged the need to bolster his confidence in the wake of a surprising quarter-final loss to American Tommy Paul at the Canadian Open. The defeat, a 6-3 4-6 6-3 setback, marked Alcaraz's first loss since the French Open semi-finals in June, striking a blow to his preparations for the upcoming U.S. Open.

Addressing reporters post-match, Carlos Alcaraz admitted to somewhat lackluster performance: "I recognize that my game wasn't up to par in these matches. I'm focusing on improving through practice ahead of the U.S. Open, which is a few weeks away."

His immediate attention, however, centers on the Cincinnati Masters 1000, considering it as a significant tournament. "I need to shift my focus to Cincinnati. It's a pivotal event, and I'm taking valuable lessons from my recent matches," he remarked.

While acknowledging some positive aspects in his game, Alcaraz emphasized the potential for improvement across the board. "There were some positives, but there's room for enhancement in every aspect. Even after my best matches, there's always room for growth," he added, highlighting his quest to enhance confidence in his game.

Looking forward, Alcaraz braces for a potential rematch with Paul in Cincinnati's third round. With the head-to-head tally at 1-2 in Paul's favor, Alcaraz recognizes the American's threat in the final warm-up event before the U.S. Open.

Praising Paul's skill set, Alcaraz described him as a well-rounded player with remarkable talent, speed, and proficiency across surfaces. "He's undeniably one of the top players globally at present. His versatility makes him a formidable opponent," acknowledged the reigning Flushing Meadows champion.
